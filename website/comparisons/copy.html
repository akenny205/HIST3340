What Does "Original" Mean?

With print, there's a meaningful distinction between an original and a copy. A first edition has value precisely because it's not a reproduction. But when every PDF is bit-for-bit identical, the concept of "original" becomes complicated.

The file on your computer is indistinguishable from the file on mine. There's no wear, no patina, no provenance written into the object itself.

Implications for Access

Instant duplicability has democratized access to texts in unprecedented ways. Academic papers, historical documents, and literature can spread globally in seconds. Yet this same feature enables piracy and challenges traditional publishing economics.

Historical Perspective

The printing press was revolutionary because it reduced the cost of copying by orders of magnitude. The PDF continues this trajectory to its logical extreme: copying becomes essentially free. This fundamentally changes the economics of knowledge distribution and raises questions about ownership, copyright, and the value of textual objects.

Software Dependency

A printed book needs only light to be read. A PDF requires electricity, hardware, an operating system, and rendering software, each layer adding complexity and potential points of failure.

The Technology Stack

To read a PDF, you need an entire ecosystem of technology working together. Each layer depends on the one below it:

Rendering Varies by Software

The same PDF file may look different depending on what software interprets it. Fonts may substitute, colors may shift, and interactive elements may not work at all. A Kindle renders a PDF very differently than Adobe Acrobat on a desktop.

Points of Failure

  • Power outage , No electricity, no reading
  • Software update , New versions may break old files
  • Format obsolescence , Will PDF readers exist in 100 years?
  • DRM restrictions , Software can refuse to open files
  • Missing fonts , Text may display incorrectly
  • Device failure , Hardware dies, access lost

The Print Advantage

A book printed 500 years ago can still be read today with no special equipment. The same cannot be said for a PDF created 5 years ago on obsolete software. Print's "technology stack" is remarkably simple: paper + ink + light + eyes.

Mediated Reading

Every PDF reading experience is mediated by layers of technology that interpret, render, and display the content. The software makes invisible decisions about how to present the text. With print, what you see is what was physically created. With PDFs, what you see is one possible interpretation of abstract data.

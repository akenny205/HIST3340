About the Class

HIST 3340: Technologies of Text

This course explores the history of textual technologies, from handwritten manuscripts to printed books to digital documents. We've examined how the material form of texts shapes how they are created, distributed, read, and preserved.

Throughout the semester, we've studied everything from medieval scribes and the printing press to the rise of zines and digital formats. The central question driving our work: How does the medium of a text shape its meaning?

This website is my Final Creative Expression Project, a chance to synthesize what I've learned and explore a topic that bridges my background in computer science with the humanistic questions at the heart of this course.

How This Website Was Built

Building a website is a lot like building a house. Different technologies play different roles, from the structure to the style to the behind-the-scenes systems that make everything work.

HTML , The Structure

Walls, rooms, and doors

HTML defines the basic structure of a webpage. Just like walls divide a house into rooms, HTML creates the sections, headings, paragraphs, and containers that organize content. It's the skeleton that holds everything together.

CSS , The Style

Paint, flooring, and interior design

CSS controls how everything looks. Colors, fonts, spacing, layouts, all the visual design choices that transform bare walls into a beautiful space. It's what gives this site its warm, scholarly aesthetic.

TypeScript , The Behavior

Lights, plumbing, and thermostats

TypeScript adds interactivity, the things that respond when you click, toggle, or scroll. Like the electrical and plumbing systems that make a house functional, TypeScript makes the interactive PDF layer demo and navigation work.

Vite , The Build Tool

Construction crew and power tools

Vite is the behind-the-scenes machinery that assembles everything during development. It's like having a construction crew with power tools, you don't see them in the finished house, but they made building it much faster and easier.

Why Build From Scratch?

Just as the materiality of a printed book matters, its paper, binding, and typography, the technology behind a website shapes its character. I built this site without heavy frameworks to maintain full control over its design, mirroring how the format of a text shapes its meaning.

04

Why This Project?

We encounter PDFs every day, but rarely think about what they actually are.

As someone who writes code daily, I wanted to investigate the hidden complexity of a format I take for granted. The PDF isn't just a "digital piece of paper", it's a sophisticated technology with its own history, assumptions, and consequences for how we read and share information.

This project bridges my technical background with the humanistic questions we've explored in class: How does format shape meaning? What do we gain and lose in the digital transition? Who controls access to texts?
